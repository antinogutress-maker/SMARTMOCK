Animated Valves Guide

Master the internal mechanics, functions, and locations of water supply valves for your Loksewa exams. Watch how they interact with the flow.

Gate Valve Sluice Valve
Primary Function

To completely shut off or start the flow for repairs. (Notice how the gate drops to block the entire pipe section.)

Ideal Location

At the start of main lines and at branch junctions.

Globe Valve
Primary Function

To "throttle" or regulate the flow rate. (The plug partially closes, squeezing the water path and slowing the flow.)

Ideal Location

Used where precise flow control is needed (e.g., public tap stands).

Air Release Valve
Primary Function

To release trapped air pockets that cause "air locks." (Air bubbles gather at the peak, pushing the float down to vent air to the atmosphere.)

Ideal Location

At all summits (highest points) of the pipeline profile.

Wash-out Valve Scour Valve
Primary Function

To drain the pipe and flush out accumulated silt/sediment. (The valve opens periodically to blow out the brown silt that settles at the bottom.)

Ideal Location

At all depressions (lowest points) of the pipeline profile.

Check Valve Non-Return
Primary Function

To allow water to flow in only one direction. (When water tries to flow backwards, the flap slams shut to block it.)

Ideal Location

On the delivery side of pumps to prevent water from emptying back into the source.

Float Valve Ball Valve
Primary Function

To automatically shut off flow when a tank is full. (As water rises, it lifts the ball, causing the lever to push a stopper into the inlet pipe.)

Ideal Location

Inside Reservoirs (RVT) and Break Pressure Tanks (BPT).

Pressure Reducing Valve
Primary Function

To automatically reduce high upstream pressure. (The turbulent, high-pressure red flow enters the valve, compresses the internal spring, and exits as a calm, low-pressure green flow.)

Ideal Location

Where pipe pressure exceeds the safe limit of the pipe (acts as a mechanical BPT).
