Irrigation Engineering Structures

Dynamic Visualizations & Loksewa Exam Notes for Hill Irrigation

1

Capture & Treatment (Headworks)

1. Intake Structure Top View

  • Definition A hydraulic structure built at the water source (river/khola) to safely divert the required amount of water into the main canal.
  • Purpose Regulates the flow into the canal and prevents large boulders, debris, and excessive floodwater from entering the system.
  • Hill Feature Side intakes or bottom-trench (Tyrolean) weirs are preferred to handle steep gradients and heavy bed loads.

2. De-silting / De-sanding Basin Cross Section

  • Definition A specially widened and deepened section of the canal constructed immediately downstream of the intake.
  • Purpose To decrease the flow velocity, allowing suspended sediments (silt and sand) to settle out of the water before entering the main canal.
  • Crucial Need Prevents the choking of canals and rapid wear-and-tear of turbine blades (in multi-purpose hydro projects). Has a flushing gate to return sediment to the river.
2

Cross Drainage (CD) Works - Grade Separated

3. Aqueduct 3D/Top View

  • Definition A CD work where the irrigation canal is carried over the natural drainage (stream/khola).
  • Condition Used when the High Flood Level (HFL) of the drainage is significantly lower than the canal bed level.
  • Purpose Acts like a bridge for the canal, allowing uninterrupted flow of both the canal water and the natural stream below.

4. Super Passage 3D/Top View

  • Definition A CD work where the natural drainage (stream) is carried over the irrigation canal.
  • Condition Used when the canal Full Supply Level (FSL) is well below the bed level of the natural drainage.
  • Purpose Safely passes torrential hill streams over the canal without mixing the waters. The canal flows in a flume below.
3

Cross Drainage (CD) Works - At-Grade & Pressure

5. Level Crossing Top View

  • Definition A CD work where the canal and the natural stream intersect at approximately the same bed level.
  • Operation Waters are allowed to mix. It requires a crest wall across the stream, a Head Regulator for the canal, and a Cross Regulator (gates) to flush the stream water during floods.
  • Purpose Used when it is too expensive to construct an aqueduct or syphon due to matching elevations.

6. Canal Syphon Cross Section

  • Definition A CD work where the canal passes under the natural drainage, and the canal water flows under hydrostatic pressure.
  • Condition Used when the canal bed is below the stream bed, but the FSL of the canal is higher than the stream bed.
  • Mechanism Functions on the principle of an inverted syphon (U-tube). Highly prone to siltation at the bottom of the dip.
4

Gradient Control & Canal Stability

7. Canal Fall (Drop) Cross Section

  • Definition A solid masonry or concrete structure constructed to lower the water level of the canal safely.
  • Condition Highly necessary in hill irrigation where the natural ground slope is much steeper than the designed bed slope of the canal.
  • Purpose To safely dissipate the excess kinetic energy generated by the drop (often creating a hydraulic jump) to prevent downstream scouring.

8. Canal Protection Works Cross Section

  • Definition Structural interventions (like Retaining walls and Breast walls) built along contour canals in hilly areas.
  • Retaining Wall Built on the downhill/valley side to support the canal bed and outer bank from slipping.
  • Breast Wall Built on the uphill/mountain side to protect the canal from debris fall, minor landslides, and slope failures.
5

Water Distribution Systems

9. Division Box (Proportional Divider) Top View

  • Definition A structure constructed at the junction where a main or branch canal splits into two or more secondary canals.
  • Purpose To accurately split and proportion the flow of water according to the design discharge of the branching canals. Often utilizes adjustable weirs or gates.

10. Outlet (Turnout) Top View

  • Definition A small structure built at the head of a watercourse (field channel) to connect it to a minor or distributary canal.
  • Purpose It is the crucial link connecting the government-managed irrigation system to the farmer-managed field channels. Delivers a specified volume of water to the command area.
