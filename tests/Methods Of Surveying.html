Survey Field Book: Page 12

  • Methods of Levelling

    (Complete Classification)

    • 1. Direct Methods

      Level instrument used
      Highly Accurate measurements
      Physical staff readings taken

      • Simple

        Elevation diff. of 2 pts
        Instrument at mid-point
        No obstacles between

      • Differential

        For distant/non-visible points
        Multiple setup stations
        Requires Change Points (CP)

      • Reciprocal

        Across Rivers/Deep Valleys
        Eliminates Collimation error
        Cancels Curvature/Refraction

      • Fly Levelling

        To check accuracy of work
        Only B.S & F.S readings taken
        Ends at known Bench Mark

      • Longitudinal

        Along the centerline of road
        For gradient fix (L-Section)
        Taken at regular intervals

      • Cross Section

        Perpendicular to centerline
        Lateral ground profile
        Used for Volume Calculations

      • Precise

        Uses Invar Staff (Stable)
        High accuracy Tilting Levels
        For Master Bench Marks

    • 2. Indirect Methods

      Level instrument NOT used
      Relative/Calculated Accuracy
      Derived via Physics & Math

      • Trigonometric

        Theodolite vertical angles
        Formula: H = D tan(α)
        For inaccessible hilly peaks

      • Barometric

        Pressure vs Altitude logic
        Uses Aneroid Altimeter
        For rapid reconnaissance

      • Hypsometric

        Water Boiling Point temp
        B.P drops 1°C per 300m alt
        For high mountain surveys
