Embankment (Levees / Marginal Bunds)

Top View (Aerial) 🚁

Cross-Section View 🔪

HFL = High Flood Level

Exam Notes

  • Definition These are earthen, trapezoidal structures constructed parallel to the riverbanks along the floodplains.
  • Primary Purpose To confine the high flood discharge within a defined cross-section of the river, preventing it from spilling over and inundating adjacent valuable agricultural lands, settlements, and infrastructure.
  • Engineering Feature They must be designed with an adequate freeboard (usually 1 to 1.5 meters above the High Flood Level) and a proper hydraulic gradient line to prevent seepage failure (piping).
2

Spur (Groyne)

Top View (Aerial)

Dots = Siltation

Cross-Section View

Exam Notes

  • Definition These are transverse structures built projecting from the riverbank into the active river channel.
  • Primary Purpose To protect the riverbank from erosion by deflecting the high-velocity water currents away from the bank and towards the central channel.
  • Mechanism By reducing the water velocity near the bank, spurs induce siltation (sediment deposition) between successive spurs, effectively rebuilding the eroded bank.
  • Types Based on alignment, they are classified as Repelling (pointing upstream), Attracting (pointing downstream), or Deflecting (perpendicular). They can be permeable (bamboo/timber) or impermeable (gabions/boulders).

Stud

Top View (Aerial)

Cross-Section View

Exam Notes

  • Definition A stud is essentially a very short spur or a localized hard point constructed flush with or slightly projecting from the riverbank.
  • Primary Purpose To provide localized protection to highly vulnerable, specific points on a bank that are subjected to direct, concentrated scouring.
  • Application In a series of longer spurs, studs are often constructed in the intermediate spaces to prevent the river from creating curved erosion pockets (embayment) between the main structures.
4

Big Boulder Riprap (Pitching)

Top View (Aerial)

Cross-Section View

Notice Scour Hole & Launching Apron

Exam Notes

  • Definition A protective layer made of large, well-graded natural stones or boulders placed over the sloping riverbanks or the riverbed.
  • Primary Purpose To absorb the hydraulic and kinetic energy of the flowing water, thereby protecting the underlying erodible soil from being washed away.
  • Engineering Feature It is a flexible structure. If the riverbed scours at the toe of the bank, the loose boulders settle into the scoured hole (acting as a launching apron), preventing the total collapse of the upper bank.

Concrete Block Riprap

Top View (Aerial)

Cross-Section View

Yellow line = Filter Layer

Exam Notes

  • Definition A heavy-duty protective lining consisting of precast concrete blocks, placed either loosely or interlocked, on the riverbanks.
  • Primary Purpose Used in high-velocity river reaches or outside curves of meandering rivers where the shear stress is too high for natural boulders to withstand the drag forces.
  • Engineering Feature It provides a highly durable, semi-rigid to rigid protection. It requires a proper filter layer (like geotextiles or graded gravel) underneath the blocks to prevent the fine subsoil from washing out through the joints.
