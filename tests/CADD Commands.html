Interactive Study Guide for Civil Engineering Surveying (Loksewa)

Drawing Commands

1. Line (L)

Draws a simple, individual straight line segment between two specified points.

2. Polyline (PL)

Creates a single, continuous object composed of interconnected straight lines and arc segments.

3. Hatch (H)

Fills an enclosed boundary or object with a specified pattern to represent materials (like concrete or brick).

4. Arc (A)

Draws a segment of a circle, typically using 3 geometric points or a start, center, and end point.

5. Circle (C)

Creates a perfectly round, closed geometric shape based on a specified center point and radius.

6. Rectangle (REC)

Quickly draws a closed, four-sided polyline by specifying two opposite diagonal corners.

7. Polygon (POL)

Creates an equilateral (equal-sided) closed polyline with a specified number of sides (e.g., a hexagon).

8. Ellipse (EL)

Draws an oval curve defined by a major (long) axis and a minor (short) axis.

Modify Commands

1. Offset (O)

Creates a parallel copy of a line, polyline, or curve at a specified distance (crucial for drawing wall thicknesses).

2. Mirror (MI)

Creates a reversed, perfectly symmetrical duplicate of an object across a defined mirror axis line.

3. Chamfer (CHA)

Connects two non-parallel intersecting lines by cutting the corner to create a flat, angled (beveled) edge.

4. Trim (TR)

Cuts and removes portions of an object that extend beyond the intersection edge of another object.

5. Extend (EX)

Lengthens a line or arc so that its endpoint perfectly touches a specified boundary object.

6. Fillet (F)

Connects two objects by replacing their sharp intersection with a smooth, rounded arc of a specified radius.
