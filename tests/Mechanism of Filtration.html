4 Mechanisms of Filtration

Loksewa Objective Trap: Filtration is NOT just a sieve! It's a combination of physical, chemical, and biological processes. Understand these 4 actions to ace the Water Quality section.

1 Mechanical Straining

The physical sieve. Sand particles are closely packed, creating tiny gaps (voids). Impurities that are larger than these gaps are physically trapped and cannot pass through. (Watch the large particle get stuck, while the tiny one slips through.)

2 Flocculation & Sedimentation

For particles smaller than the gaps. As water flows through the winding voids, the velocity changes. Fine particles slow down, bump into each other (flocculate), and gently settle onto the surface of the sand grains due to gravity.

3 Biological Action

The most crucial purification step. A sticky, slimy layer of harmless bacteria called the Schmutzdecke ("dirty skin") forms on the top layer of sand. This layer actively "eats" and destroys harmful pathogenic bacteria. (Highly active in Slow Sand Filters).

4 Electrolytic Action

Sand grains and water impurities often hold opposite electrical charges. Because opposite charges attract, the fine colloidal impurities are drawn to the sand grains like magnets. Upon contact, their charges neutralize, causing them to stick firmly to the sand.
