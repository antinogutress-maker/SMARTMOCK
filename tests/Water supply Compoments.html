Water Supply Engineering: Core Structures

Loksewa Sub-Engineer Exam Preparation (Syllabus 11.2.10)

1. Collection Chamber

A water-retaining structure built immediately downstream of a spring intake.

  • Function: Collects water from source points, allows fine particles to settle, and submerges the outlet pipe to prevent air entry.
  • Location: Just below the intake, before the transmission main.
Exam Tip: Always mention it must include a ventilation pipe and an overflow pipe.

2. Break Pressure Tank (BPT)

A small tank used to reduce hydrostatic pressure in the pipeline to zero (atmospheric).

  • Function: Prevents static pressure from exceeding pipe capacity and bursting pipes by breaking the continuous water column.
  • Location: At calculated elevations where static head approaches max pressure rating (every 60m-100m vertical drop).
Exam Tip: The key phrase to write is "Discharges water to the atmosphere to reset hydraulic grade line to zero."

3. Wash-out Valve (Scour Valve)

A valve installed to drain the pipeline for maintenance.

  • Function: Flushes accumulated sediment/silt and empties the pipe section for safe repairs.
  • Location: Always at the lowest points (depressions or valleys) of the pipeline profile.
Exam Tip: Air valves = summits. Wash-out valves = depressions.

4. Air Valve

A mechanical valve that automatically releases or admits air.

  • Function: Releases trapped air bubbles to prevent "air locks" and admits air during drainage to prevent vacuum collapse.
  • Location: Always installed at the highest points (summits or peaks) of the pipeline.

5. Valve Chamber

A protective masonry or concrete box built around pipeline valves.

  • Function: Protects valves from damage, tampering, and weather while allowing operator access.
  • Key Features: Must have a lockable cover and proper bottom drainage to prevent flooding.

6. Reservoir Tank (RVT)

The primary storage structure in a gravity system.

  • Function: Balances constant source inflow with fluctuating hourly community demand. Provides emergency storage.
  • Design Concept: Sized based on "Balancing Capacity" calculated via mass curve.

7. Division Box

A structure used to split main water flow proportionally.

  • Function: Distributes water fairly to different wards based on population using V-notch weirs or orifices.
  • Location: At junctions where the transmission main splits into distribution branches.

8. Tap Stand Post

The final delivery point of the water supply system.

  • Function: Dispenses water safely and hygienically to the public.
  • Components: Concrete pillar, standing platform, catch drain, and a soak pit to dispose of wastewater safely.
